Chapter 6 — System of Particles & Rotational Motion

Arguably the toughest chapter of Class 11, but also the highest weightage (12–15 marks). Master centre of mass motion, moment of inertia tables, parallel & perpendicular axis theorems, and pure rolling condition — you’ll dominate the exam.

Party wala tip: 90 % numericals are from moment of inertia + torque + rolling. Memorise the MI table by heart.

1. Centre of Mass (COM)

For particles: Xcm = (Σ mᵢ xᵢ)/(Σ mᵢ)  Ycm = (Σ mᵢ yᵢ)/(Σ mᵢ)

Velocity of COM: v⃗cm = (Σ mᵢ v⃗ᵢ)/M
Acceleration: a⃗cm = F⃗ext / M

2. Moment of Inertia (Must Memorise)

Thin rod (about centre ⊥)       → (1/12)ML²
Thin rod (about end ⊥)          → (1/3)ML²
Ring/Disc (about centre ⊥)      → MR²
Solid sphere                     → (2/5)MR²
Hollow sphere                   → (2/3)MR²
Solid cylinder (axis)           → (1/2)MR²
Rectangular lamina (centre)      → (1/12)M(a²+b²)

3. Parallel & Perpendicular Axis Theorems

Parallel axis: I = Icm + M d²
Perpendicular axis (planar): Iz = Ix + Iy

4. Torque & Angular Momentum

τ⃗ = r⃗ × F⃗  τ = r F sinθ
L⃗ = r⃗ × p⃗  For rigid body L = I ω
Conservation: If τ⃗ext = 0 → L constant

5. Pure Rolling Condition

vcm = R ω  acm = R α

For rolling without slipping on horizontal surface:
f ≤ μ N  and a = g sinθ / (1 + I/(MR²)) 
Solid sphere   → k²/R² = 2/5 → a = (5/7)g sinθ
Hollow sphere  → k²/R² = 2/3 → a = (5/8)g sinθ
Ring           → k²/R² = 1   → a = (1/2)g sinθ

6. High-Weightage Solved Numericals

Q1: Two point masses 3 kg at (0,0) and 5 kg at (4,0). Find COM.

Xcm = (3×0 + 5×4)/(8) = 2.5 m

Q2: MI of a thin rod 2 kg, length 1 m about an axis through its end perpendicular to length.

I = (1/3)ML² = (1/3)×2×1 = 2/3 kg m²

Q3: Disc (R=0.1 m, M=2 kg) has Icm = ½MR². Find MI about tangent.

I = ½MR² + MR² = (3/2)MR² = 1.5×2×0.01 = 0.03 kg m²

Q4: Solid sphere rolling down 30° incline. Acceleration?

a = (5/7)g sin30° = (5/7)×10×0.5 = 25/7 ≈ 3.57 m/s²

Q5: A ring released from top of incline (h = 5 m. Find speed at bottom.

mgh = ½mv² + ½Iω² → mgh = ½mv² + ½(mR²)(v/R)² → v = √(gh) = √50 = 5√2 m/s

7. Practice Questions

  1. Find MI of a square plate side a about axis through centre parallel to side.
  2. A cylinder rolls purely. Ratio v/ω = ?
  3. Find velocity of a hollow sphere at bottom if released from height 14 m.
  4. Torque of 20 Nm applied on body I=5 kg m² for 4 s from rest. Final ω?

(Answers): 1) Ma²/6 2) R 3) 20 m/s 4) 16 rad/s

8. Quick Formula Sheet

COM:               x_cm = Σmᵢxᵢ/M
Parallel axis:     I = I_cm + Md²
Rolling a =        g sinθ / (1 + k²/R²)
Sphere (solid) →   5/7 g sinθ
Ring/hollow cyl →  1/2 g sinθ
Angular momentum   L = Iω = constant (if τ_ext=0)
Torque             τ = Iα
Work by torque     W = τ θ
Power              P = τ ω
