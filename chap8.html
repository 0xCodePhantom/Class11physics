Chapter 8 — Mechanical Properties of Solids

Short but very very scoring chapter (6–8 marks almost every year). Just three moduli, stress-strain curve, and elastic energy — bas itna ratt lo, full marks pakke.

Board Exam Mantra: Formula of Bulk modulus aur elastic energy wala question 100 % aata hai!

1. Stress & Strain

Stress  σ = Force / Area          (N/m² or Pascal)
Strain  ε = ΔL / L  (dimensionless)

Types of stress:
   • Tensile / Compressive  → longitudinal
   • Shear                  → tangential
   • Hydraulic / Volumetric → pressure

2. Hooke’s Law & Elastic Moduli

Within elastic limit: stress ∝ strain ⇒ stress = E × strain

Young’s modulus  Y = (F/A) / (ΔL/L) = FL / (A ΔL)
Bulk modulus    B = –P / (ΔV/V)  (negative sign because volume decreases)
Rigidity modulus G = (F/A) / θ  (shear strain θ = x/l) 
Relation: Y = 3B = 2G × (something) → actually Y = 2G(1+ν)G = 3(1−2ν)B
Poisson’s ratio ν = lateral strain / longitudinal strain (0 < ν < 0.5)

3. Stress-Strain Curve (Must Draw in Exam)

Proportional limit → Hooke’s law valid
Elastic limit      → beyond this permanent deformation
Yield point        → plastic flow starts
Ultimate tensile strength → max stress material can bear
Breaking point
Steel → large elastic region, Ductile
Rubber → large strain, low Young’s modulus

4. Elastic Potential Energy in Wire

Energy stored per unit volume U = ½ × stress × strain
Total energy in wire U = ½ × (F × ΔL) or (½) Y (ΔL)² A / L

5. Favourite Board Numericals

Q1: Steel wire (Y=2×10¹¹ Pa) length 2 m, area 0.1 cm² stretched by 1 mm. Find force.

Y = FL/(AΔL) ⇒ F = Y A ΔL / L = 2×10¹¹ × 10⁻⁵ × 10⁻³ / 2 = 1000 N

Q2: A rubber cord volume 100 cm³ subjected to pressure 10⁶ Pa. Bulk modulus 2×10⁹ Pa. Find ΔV.

B = –P/(ΔV/V) ⇒ ΔV = –P V / B = –10⁶ × 10⁻⁴ / 2×10⁹ = –0.05 cm³

Q3: Wire stretched by 0.1%. Energy stored per unit volume if Y=2×10¹¹ Pa?

U = ½ stress × strain = ½ Y ε² = ½ × 2×10¹¹ × (0.001)² = 10⁵ J/m³

Q4: Two wires same material, length ratio 1:2, area ratio 2:1. Ratio of extensions under same force?

ΔL ∝ L/A ⇒ ratio = (L₁/L₂)×(A₂/A₁) = (1/2)×(2 = 1:1

6. Practice Questions

  1. Steel rod (Y=2×10¹¹ Pa) diameter 1 mm bears 500 N load. Elongation if length 3 m?
  2. Find energy stored in a wire stretched by 0.2% (Y=10¹¹ Pa).
  3. Bulk modulus of water 2.2×10⁹ Pa. % decrease in volume under 100 atm pressure?
  4. Maximum load a copper wire (Y=1.1×10¹¹ Pa, area 1 mm²) can bear without crossing elastic limit (safe strain 0.2%).

(Answers): 1) 2.86 mm 2) 2×10⁵ J/m³ 3) 0.463% 4) 220 N

7. One-Page Formula Sheet

Y = FL / (A ΔL)  ⇒ ΔL = FL / (AY)
B = –P V / ΔV  ⇒ ΔV = –P V / B
G = (F/A) / θ
Elastic energy density = ½ stress × strain = ½ Y ε²
Work done in stretching = ½ F × ΔL
For same material & load: ΔL ∝ L/A
