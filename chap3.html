Introduction — Motion in Two Dimensions

Chapter 3 extends kinematics to two dimensions. We use vectors for displacement, velocity and acceleration. The two most important examples are projectile motion and uniform circular motion. Master vector resolution, time of flight, range, and centripetal acceleration — these topics dominate board exams.

Golden Rule: Resolve every vector into x and y components. Motion in x-direction is always independent of y-direction (no acceleration in x for projectile).

1. Vector Basics (Must Revise)

Addition          → Parallelogram / Triangle law
Subtraction       → Same with negative vector
Dot product       → A·B = |A||B|cosθ
Cross product    → |A×B| = |A||B|sinθ
Unit vectors      → î, ĵ, k̂
Resolution        → Aₓ = A cosθ,   Aᵧ = A sinθ
Resultant of two vectors at angle θ:
R = √(A² + B² + 2AB cosθ)

2. Projectile Motion (Most Important Topic)

Object projected at angle θ with initial velocity u.

uₓ = u cosθ  (unchanged throughout)
uᵧ = u sinθ  (decelerates by g)

Time of flight T = 2u sinθ / g
Maximum height H = (u sinθ)² / (2g)
Range    R = u² sin2θ / g  (max when θ = 45°)
Range is same for θ and (90°–θ)
Equation of trajectory (eliminate t):
y = x tanθ – (g x²)/(2 u² cos²θ)

Key Results Table (g = 10 m/s²)

θ      | 30°    45°    60°
────────────────────────────
sin2θ  |  √3/2   1     √3/2
R (u²/g)|  √3 u²/g  u²/g  √3 u²/g
H      |  u²/8   u²/4   3u²/8

3. Relative Velocity in Plane

v⃗_A,B = v⃗_A – v⃗_B
Example: Boat velocity 8 m/s due east, river flows 6 m/s north. Find velocity of boat w.r.t. ground.

v⃗_bg = 8î + 6ĵ
|v⃗_bg| = √(64+36) = 10 m/s at tan⁻¹(6/8) = 37° north of east.

4. Uniform Circular Motion

Angular velocity ω = v/r = 2π/T = 2πf
Centripetal acceleration a_c = v²/r = ω²r (towards centre)
Centripetal force F_c = mv²/r
Velocity is tangential, but acceleration is always radial (perpendicular to velocity). No tangential acceleration in uniform circular motion.

5. Important Solved Examples (Board Level)

Q1: A projectile is fired at 30° with 40 m/s. Find (g=10 m/s²):
(a) Time of flight (b) Max height (c) Range

T = 2×40×0.5 /10 = 4 s
H = (40×0.5)²/(2×10) = 20 m
R = 40² × sin60° /10 = 80√3 m ≈ 138.6 m

Q2: Find angle for maximum range with speed 50 m/s.

Maximum range when sin2θ = 1 ⇒ 2θ = 90° ⇒ θ = 45°

Q3: Stone thrown horizontally from 45 m height with 15 m/s. Find time to hit ground and horizontal distance.

For vertical: s = ½gt² ⇒ 45 = 5t² ⇒ t = 3 s
Horizontal distance = 15 × 3 = 45 m

Q4 (Circular): Car takes 20 s to complete a circular track of radius 70 m. Find centripetal acceleration.

C = 2πr = 440 m
v = 440/20 = 22 m/s
a_c = v²/r = 484/70 ≈ 6.91 m/s²

Q5 (Relative): Rain falls vertically at 10 m/s. Man walks east at 6 m/s. Find velocity of rain w.r.t. man.

v⃗_rain,man = 0î – 10ĵ – 6î = –6î – 10ĵ
Speed = √(36+100) = 11.66 m/s at tan⁻¹(10/6) below west.

6. Practice Questions

  1. A ball is projected at 53° with 20 m/s. Find range (sin53°=0.8, cos53°=0.6, g=10).
  2. Two projectiles have same range. If one is at 20°, find angle of other.
  3. A particle moves in circle of radius 5 m with constant speed 10 m/s. Find centripetal acceleration.
  4. From a 80 m tower, a stone is thrown horizontally at 10 m/s. Where does it hit ground?

(Answers): 1) 40 m 2) 70° 3) 20 m/s² 4) 40 m from base

7. Formula Sheet — Memorize These

Projectile:
T = 2u sinθ / g
H = u² sin²θ / (2g)
R = u² sin2θ / g
Horizontal projection:
t = √(2h/g),   R = u √(2h/g)

Circular:
v = rω
a_c = v²/r = ω²r
T = 2πr/v

Relative velocity:
v⃗_A,B = v⃗_A,G – v⃗_B,G

8. Exam Tips & Common Mistakes

  • Never use g in horizontal direction for projectile
  • For maximum range → θ = 45°
  • Time of flight depends only on vertical component
  • In circular motion, acceleration is v²/r, not tangential
  • Always convert km/h to m/s (×5/18)
  • Draw vector diagram for relative motion in 2D
