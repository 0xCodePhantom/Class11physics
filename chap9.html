Chapter 9 — Mechanical Properties of Fluids

One of the most beautiful & numerical-heavy chapters. Topics: hydrostatic pressure, Archimedes, Bernoulli, viscosity & surface tension. 10–12 marks every year — learn the formulas by heart and practise 20 numericals.

Board Favourite: Bernoulli + continuity equation, U-tube manometer, buoyancy, terminal velocity, excess pressure in drop/bubble.

1. Pressure in Fluids

P = F/A  (Pascal)
Hydrostatic pressure at depth h: P = P₀ + ρgh
Pascal’s law: Pressure applied is transmitted equally in all directions.

2>2. Archimedes’ Principle & Buoyancy

Buoyant force F_B = Weight of displaced fluid = V_displaced × ρ_fluid × g
Apparent weight = Actual weight – F_B
Object floats if ρ_object < ρ_fluid
Sinks if ρ_object > ρ_fluid
Neutral if equal

3. Bernoulli’s Equation (Most Important)

P + ½ ρ v² + ρ g h = constant  (along a streamline)
Speed increases → pressure decreases (Venturi, aerofoil lift, atomiser)

4. Viscosity & Stokes’ Law

Stokes’ law: F = 6πηrv
Terminal velocity v_t = 2r²(ρ−σ)g / (9η)

5. Surface Tension

Excess pressure inside soap bubble ΔP = 4T/R
Inside liquid drop ΔP = 2T/R
Capillary rise h = 2T cosθ / (ρ g r)

6. Board-Level Solved Numericals

Q1: U-tube contains mercury. 10 cm height difference. Find pressure difference.

ΔP = ρgh = 13600 × 10 × 0.1 = 13600 Pa

Q2: A cube 10 cm side, density 0.8 g/cm³ in water. Find apparent weight.

V = 0.001 m³, F_B = 0.001 × 1000 × 10 = 10 N
Actual wt = 0.8 × 1000 × 0.001 ×10 = 8 N
Apparent wt = –2 N (floats upward)

Q3 (Bernoulli): Water flows through pipe, area 4 cm² → 1 cm², height same. Find pressure drop.

v₁A₁ = v₂A₂ ⇒ v₂ = 4v₁
P₁ – P₂ = ½ρ(v₂² – v₁²) = ½×1000×15v₁² = 7500 v₁² Pa

Q4: Soap bubble radius 2 cm, T=0.04 N/m. Excess pressure?

ΔP = 4T/R = 4×0.04/0.02 = 8 Pa

Q5: Steel ball (r=1 mm, ρ=8000 kg/m³) in glycerine η=0.9 Pa s. Terminal velocity?

v_t = 2(0.001)²(8000−1260)×9.8/(9×0.9) ≈ 0.015 m/s

7. Practice Questions

  1. Hydraulic lift: area ratio 1:100, effort 200 N. Load lifted?
  2. Find excess pressure in air bubble of radius 0.1 mm in water (T=0.073 N/m).
  3. Speed of efflux from 4 m deep tank (Torricelli).
  4. Capillary rise of water in tube radius 0.1 mm (T=0.075 N/m, θ=0).

(Answers): 1) 20,000 N 2) 2920 Pa 3) √80 ≈ 8.94 m/s 4) 15 cm
