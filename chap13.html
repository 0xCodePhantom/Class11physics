Chapter 13 — Oscillations

Beautiful chapter with 10–14 marks weightage. Almost every year one 5-mark numerical on spring/pendulum combo. Master T formulas and energy expressions — full marks guaranteed!

Board Hack: 90 % numericals are from spring block, combined springs, or simple pendulum.

1. Simple Harmonic Motion (SHM)

a = –ω²x  ⇒ ω = 2π/T = 2πf
x = A sin(ωt + φ) or A cos(ωt + φ) 
v = ω √(A² – x²)  v_max = ωA
a_max = ω²A

2. Spring Mass System

T = 2π √(m/k)  (single spring)
Series: k_eff = k₁k₂/(k₁+k₂)  T = 2π √(m(k₁+k₂)/(k₁k₂))
Parallel: k_eff = k₁ + k₂  T = 2π √(m/(k₁+k₂))

3. Simple Pendulum

T = 2π √(L/g)  (for small amplitude)
At depth d: T = 2π √(L/(g(1–d/R)))

4. Energy in SHM (Very Important)

KE = ½ m ω² (A² – x²)
PE = ½ m ω² x² or ½ k x²
Total E = ½ m ω² A² = ½ k A² = constant

5. Damped & Forced Oscillations

Damped: amplitude decreases exponentially
Resonance: when ω_driving = ω_natural → amplitude max

6. High-Weightage Solved Numericals

Q1: Mass 2 kg, spring k=200 N/m. Time period?

T = 2π √(m/k) = 2π √(2/200) = 0.628 s

Q2: Two springs k=100 N/m each in series with 1 kg mass. T?

k_eff = 50 N/m → T = 2π √(1/50) = 0.628 s

Q3: A spring cut into 2:3 ratio. Ratio of time periods?

k ∝ 1/l → k₁:k₂ = 3:2 → T₁/T₂ = √(k₂/k₁) = √(2/3) = √2 : √3

Q4: Simple pendulum T=2 s on earth. On moon (g/6)?

T_moon = T_earth √(g/g_moon) = 2√6 ≈ 4.9 s

Q5: SHM: A=5 cm, T=4 s, find velocity at x=3 cm.

ω=π/2 rad/s → v = ω√(A²–x²) = (π/2)√(25–9) = 4 cm/s

7. Practice Questions

  1. Two springs 200 N/m and 800 N/m in parallel with 4 kg. T?
  2. Pendulum clock fast by 10 s/day on earth. Time loss on moon?
  3. Max KE in SHM of amplitude 10 cm, k=100 N/m?
  4. Find frequency when spring is cut into three equal parts.

(Answers): 1) 0.418 s 2) 50 s loss/day 3) 0.05 J 4) √3 times original
