Chapter 11 — Thermodynamics

One of the most important and scoring chapters (10–14 marks). Concepts are crystal clear, numericals are direct. Master First law sign convention, Carnot efficiency, and P-V diagrams — full marks pakke!

Golden Rule: ΔU = Q – W (IUPAC convention) → heat added positive, work done by system positive.

1. Zeroth & First Law

Zeroth law → Thermal equilibrium → defines temperature
First law  → ΔU = Q – W   (work by system positive)
           → ΔU = Q + W   (old NCERT convention – beware!)

2. Types of Processes

Isothermal (ΔT=0)     → ΔU = 0 → Q = W
Adiabatic (Q=0)       → ΔU = –W
Isochoric (ΔV=0)      → W = 0 → ΔU = Q
Isobaric (ΔP=0)       → W = PΔV
Isothermal: W = nRT ln(V₂/V₁)
Adiabatic: W = (P₁V₁ – P₂V₂)/(γ–1) = nR(T₁–T₂)/(γ–1)

3. Heat Engines & Carnot Cycle

Efficiency η = W/Q₁ = 1 – Q₂/Q₁ = 1 – T₂/T₁ Carnot theorem: No engine can have efficiency > Carnot engine

4. Second Law of Thermodynamics

Kelvin-Planck: Impossible to convert all heat into work
Clausius: Heat flows from hot to cold spontaneously
Entropy S: ΔS ≥ 0 for irreversible, ΔS = 0 for reversible

5. Board-Level Solved Numericals

Q1: Gas expands isothermally at 300 K doing 200 J work. Find Q and ΔU.

ΔT=0 → ΔU=0 → Q = W = 200 J

Q2: In adiabatic compression, temp rises from 300 K to 400 K (γ=1.4). Work done on gas?

W = –ΔU = –nCᵥΔT → |W| = n×(5/2)R×100 = depends on n, but formula used

Q3: Carnot engine operates between 600 K and 300 K. Efficiency? If Q₁=1000 J, find W and Q₂.

η = 1 – 300/600 = 50 %
W = 500 J, Q₂ = 500 J

Q4: 2 mole ideal gas (γ=1.4) compressed adiabatically, volume becomes 1/8. Final pressure if initial 1 atm?

P₂/P₁ = (V₁/V₂)^γ = 8^{1.4} = 8^{7/5} = (2³)^{7/5} = 2^{21/5} ≈ 22.6 atm

Q5: A system absorbs 500 J heat and does 200 J work. ΔU?

ΔU = Q – W = 500 – 200 = +300 J

6. Practice Questions

  1. Gas does 300 J work isothermally. Heat supplied?
  2. Carnot engine efficiency 40%. Temp ratio T₁:T₂?
  3. Adiabatic expansion: T falls from 400 K to 300 K (γ=5/3). Work done by gas?
  4. Find max possible efficiency between 1000 K and 300 K.

(Answers): 1) 300 J 2) 5:3 3) nR×100 4) 70 %

7. One-Page Formula Sheet

First law           ΔU = Q – W
Isothermal          ΔU=0, Q=W=nRT ln(V₂/V₁)
Adiabatic           Q=0, ΔU=–W, TV^{γ–1}=const, PV^γ=const
Isobaric            W=PΔV
Isochoric           W=0
Carnot efficiency   η = 1 – T₂/T₁
Work in cycle       W = area of P-V diagram
Molar heat capacity C = Cᵥ + R (isobaric)
γ = Cₚ/Cᵥ
