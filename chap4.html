Class 11 Physics — Chapter 4 — Laws of Motion

Newton’s laws • force • free-body diagram • tension • pulley systems • friction • pseudo force • solved numericals

Introduction — Dynamics Begins Here

Chapter 4 introduces the concept of force and Newton’s three laws of motion — the foundation of classical mechanics. This chapter is extremely important for both theory (10–12 marks) and numericals (pulleys, inclined plane, friction).

Most Important Skill: Drawing accurate Free-Body Diagrams (FBD). 90 % of mistakes happen because of wrong FBD.

1. Newton’s Three Laws of Motion

1st Law (Inertia)   → Body continues in its state of rest or uniform motion unless acted upon by net external force.

2nd Law             → F⃗_net = m a⃗      (F = dp/dt in general)

3rd Law             → Every action has equal and opposite reaction.
Momentum p = m v
Impulse J = Δp = F Δt

2. Common Forces You Must Know

Weight                → mg (downward)
Normal force          → N or R (perpendicular to surface)
Tension               → T (along string, same magnitude on both sides in ideal string)
Friction (static)     → fₛ ≤ μₛ N
Friction (kinetic)    → fₖ = μₖ N
Pseudo force          → –m a⃗ (in non-inertial frame)

3. Constraint Relations (Pulley Systems)

For fixed pulley: a₁ = a₂ (magnitude)
For movable pulley: a₁ = 2 a₂
Atwood machine: a = (m₁ – m₂)g / (m₁ + m₂)
Tension T = 2 m₁ m₂ g / (m₁ + m₂)

4. Friction — Static & Kinetic

Limiting friction    f_max = μₛ N
Kinetic friction     fₖ = μₖ N  (μₖ < μₛ)
Angle of repose      θ = tan⁻¹ μₛ
Minimum force to move block on rough surface at angle α:
F_min = μ N / (1 + μ tanα)  when applied at angle α with horizontal

5. Forces in Circular Motion

Banking of roads:    tanθ = v² / (r g)
Vehicle on convex bridge:  N = mg – mv²/r (at top)
Vehicle on concave bridge: N = mg + mv²/r

6. High-Weightage Solved Numericals

Q1: Two masses 5 kg and 3 kg connected by string over pulley. Find acceleration and tension (g=10 m/s²).

a = (5–3)/(5+3)g = 2g/8 = 2.5 m/s²
T = 2×5×3×10 / 8 = 37.5 N

Q2: 10 kg block on surface μ = 0.3. Minimum force to just move it?

F = μ mg = 0.3 × 10 × 10 = 30 N

Q3: Block 4 kg on 37° incline (tan37°=3/4=0.75, μ=0.2). Will it slide down?

Component down plane = mg sin37° = 40 × 0.6 = 24 N
Max friction = μ N = 0.2 × 40 × 0.8 = 6.4 N
24 N > 6.4 N ⇒ yes, it slides down

Q4: Car of mass 1000 kg moves at 54 km/h on a level road with μ=0.5. Braking distance?

v = 15 m/s, a = –μg = –5 m/s²
v² = u² + 2as ⇒ 0 = 225 – 10 s ⇒ s = 22.5 m

Q5 (Pseudo force): Lift accelerating upward at 2 m/s² with 60 kg man. Apparent weight?

N – mg = ma ⇒ N = m(g+a) = 60(10+2) = 720 N

7. Practice Questions (Exam Favourite)

  1. Two masses 8 kg and 2 kg are connected. Find acceleration and tension.
  2. A 5 kg block is pulled by 20 N at 30° to horizontal (μ=0.2). Find acceleration.
  3. Find angle of banking for safe turn (v=72 km/h, r=200 m, μ=0).
  4. A monkey of 20 kg climbs a rope that can withstand 300 N. Max acceleration upward?

(Answers): 1) a=6 m/s², T=32 N 2) 1.4 m/s² 3) ≈37° 4) 5 m/s²

8. Formula Sheet — Memorize These

Atwood:     a = (m₁-m₂)g/(m₁+m₂)  T = 2m₁m₂g/(m₁+m₂)

Incline:   Down plane → mg sinθ – friction
           Up plane   → applied – mg sinθ – friction

Friction:  fₛ ≤ μₛ N  fₖ = μₖ N

Banking:   tanθ = v²/(rg)

Pseudo force (accelerating frame): F_pseudo = –m a_frame

Apparent weight in lift:
Accelerating up   → m(g+a)
Accelerating down → m(g–a)
Free fall         → 0

9. Exam Tips & Common Mistakes

  • Always draw FBD for each body separately
  • Tension is same throughout ideal string
  • In pulley, movable pulley has acceleration half of the string acceleration
  • For banking, no friction → tanθ = v²/rg
  • Pseudo force is always opposite to acceleration of frame
  • Normal force N ≤ mg cosθ on incline
