Introduction — Kinematics in One Dimension

This chapter deals with the description of motion along a straight line (rectilinear motion). We study position, displacement, velocity, acceleration, and the famous three equations of motion for constant acceleration. Mastering graphs and sign convention is the key to scoring full marks in this chapter.

Pro Tip: Always choose a positive direction (usually to the right). Stick to it throughout the problem. Most sign-related mistakes happen because of inconsistent conventions.

1. Key Definitions

Position (x)          → location of particle w.r.t. chosen origin
Displacement (Δx)      → change in position = x₂ – x₁ (vector)
Distance               → total path length (scalar)
Speed                  → rate of distance covered (scalar)
Velocity (v)           → rate of change of displacement (vector)
Average velocity       → Δx / Δt
Instantaneous velocity → dx/dt = limit Δt→0 (Δx/Δt)
Acceleration (a)       → rate of change of velocity = dv/dt (vector)
v_avg = (v_initial + v_final)/2 (only when acceleration is constant)

2. Equations of Motion for Constant Acceleration

These three equations are the backbone of the chapter. Memorize them with their standard symbols:

v = u + at
s = ut + ½ at²
v² = u² + 2as

sₙₜₕ = u + a/2 (2n-1) (displacement in nth second)
u → initial velocity
v → final velocity
a → acceleration
t → time
s → displacement

Quick derivation reminder (using calculus)

a = dv/dt  ⇒  v = u + at
v = ds/dt  ⇒  s = ∫v dt = ut + ½ at²
Eliminate t → v² = u² + 2as

3. Motion Graphs — Must Know

Graphs are heavily tested in exams. Remember:

  • Slope of x-t graph → velocity
  • Slope of v-t graph → acceleration
  • Area under v-t graph → displacement
  • Area under a-t graph → change in velocity
Constant acceleration → v-t graph is a straight line
Uniform motion → x-t graph is straight line (constant slope = constant velocity)

4. Relative Velocity in One Dimension

If two objects A and B are moving along the same line:

v_A,B = v_A – v_B

Sign convention is critical here.

Example: A car moving at 20 m/s east overtakes a truck moving at 12 m/s east.
Relative velocity of car w.r.t. truck = 20 – 12 = 8 m/s (towards east).

5. Motion under Gravity (Free Fall)

Take downward direction as positive when object is falling.

u = 0 (if dropped)
a = g = 9.8 m/s² ≈ 10 m/s² (often used in problems)
Maximum height (when thrown up): v = 0 at top
Time of flight (thrown up): t = 2u/g
Velocity when it returns to same height = –u (magnitude same, direction opposite)

6. Important Worked Examples (Exam-Level)

Q1: A body starts from rest and moves with acceleration 4 m/s² for 5 s. Find final velocity and distance covered.

u = 0, a = 4 m/s², t = 5 s
v = u + at = 0 + 4×5 = 20 m/s
s = ut + ½at² = 0 + ½×4×25 = 50 m

Q2: A ball is thrown upward with 30 m/s. Find (i) max height (ii) time of flight (g=10 m/s²).

At max height, v = 0
v² = u² + 2as ⇒ 0 = 900 – 2×10×h ⇒ h = 45 m
Total time = 2u/g = 2×30/10 = 6 s

Q3: A train accelerates from 36 km/h to 72 km/h in 10 s. Find acceleration and distance.

First convert: 36 km/h = 10 m/s, 72 km/h = 20 m/s
a = (20-10)/10 = 1 m/s²
s = ut + ½at² = 10×10 + ½×1×100 = 150 m

Q4 (Graph-based): A particle moves with constant velocity 10 m/s for 4 s, then decelerates at 2 m/s² till rest. Find total distance.

Time to stop: v = u + at ⇒ 0 = 10 – 2t ⇒ t = 5 s
Distance in first 4 s = 10×4 = 40 m
Distance in next 5 s = (10²)/(2×2) = 25 m
Total = 65 m

7. Practice Questions (Try First!)

  1. A stone is dropped from a tower 80 m high. How long does it take to reach ground? (g=10 m/s²)
  2. A car moving at 54 km/h stops in 5 s. Find retardation and stopping distance.
  3. From v-t graph: velocity increases linearly from 0 to 20 m/s in 4 s, remains constant for 6 s, then decreases to zero in 4 s. Find total displacement.
  4. A body covers 40 m in 4th second and 60 m in 6th second. Find initial velocity and acceleration.

(Short answers): 1) 4 s  2) 3 m/s², 37.5 m  3) 200 m  4) u = 10 m/s, a = 10 m/s²

8. Quick Reference — All Formulas

v = u + at
s = ut + ½ at²
v² = u² + 2as
sₙ = u + a/2 (2n–1)
Relative velocity: v_A,B = v_A – v_B
For projectile thrown vertically up:
H_max = u²/(2g)  T_total = 2u/g

9. Exam Tips & Common Mistakes

  • Never forget to convert km/h → m/s (multiply by 5/18)
  • While taking g = 10 m/s², be consistent throughout the question
  • In relative motion, if both objects move in same direction, subtract velocities
  • Always check whether the question asks for distance or displacement
  • For stopping problems, final velocity v = 0
