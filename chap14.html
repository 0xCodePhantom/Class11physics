Chapter 14 — Waves (Last Chapter!)

The grand finale — 12–15 marks almost every year. Standing waves in strings & pipes + Doppler effect are absolute favourites. Memorise the frequency formulas and node-antinode positions — full marks confirmed!

Board 100 % Questions: Fundamental frequency of string/pipe, beats, Doppler (source moving, observer moving), speed of transverse wave.

1. Wave Equation & Speed

y = A sin(kx – ωt)  or A sin(2π(x/λ – t/T))
Wave speed v = f λ = √(T/μ) (string)  v = √(γP/ρ) (gas)

2. Standing Waves on String (Fixed Ends)

Fundamental  f₁ = (1/(2L)) √(T/μ)
nth harmonic fₙ = n f₁
Number of loops = n

3. Organ Pipes

Closed pipe (one end open):
Fundamental  f₁ = v/(4L)
Odd harmonics only: fₙ = (2n–1) f₁

Open pipe (both ends open):
Fundamental  f₁ = v/(2L)
All harmonics: fₙ = n f₁
End correction ≈ 0.3d (usually ignored in boards)

4. Beats

Beat frequency = |f₁ – f₂|

5. Doppler Effect (Most Important)

f' = f (v ± vₒ)/(v ± vₛ)
vₒ → + if observer towards source
vₛ → + if source away from observer 
Common cases (v << speed of sound):
Source moving towards observer     → f' = f (v/(v – vₛ))
Observer moving towards source     → f' = f (v + vₒ)/v
Both moving same direction         → f' = f (v + vₒ)/(v + vₛ)

6. Board Favourite Solved Numericals

Q1: String length 1 m, μ=0.01 kg/m, tension 100 N. Fundamental frequency?

v = √(100/0.01)=100 m/s → f₁ = v/(2L) = 100/2 = 50 Hz

Q2: Closed pipe 0.5 m. Speed of sound 340 m/s. First three frequencies?

f₁ = 340/(4×0.5)=170 Hz → 170, 510, 850 Hz

Q3: Two tuning forks 256 Hz and 262 Hz. Beat frequency?

|262–256| = 6 beats/sec

Q4 (Doppler): Source 500 Hz moving at 20 m/s towards stationary observer (v_sound=340 m/s).

f' = 500 × (340/(340–20)) = 500 × 340/320 ≈ 531 Hz

Q5: Open pipe resonates at 3rd harmonic 600 Hz. Length of pipe?

fₙ = n v/(2L) → 600 = 3 × v/(2L) → L = (3×340)/(2×600) = 0.85 m

7. Practice Questions

  1. Ratio of fundamental frequency of open to closed pipe of same length?
  2. A train sounding 500 Hz approaches at 36 km/h. Apparent frequency? (v=340 m/s)
  3. Sonometer wire frequency increases from 200 to 250 Hz when tension increased by 56%. Original tension?
  4. Two open pipes of length 40 cm and 41 cm. Beat frequency? (v=330 m/s)

(Answers): 1) 2:1 2) ≈ 522 Hz 3) 2.56 times 4) ≈ 4 Hz

8. One-Page Formula Sheet

Speed on string           v = √(T/μ)
String fixed both ends    fₙ = n/(2L) √(T/μ)
Open pipe                 fₙ = n v/(2L)
Closed pipe               fₙ = (2n–1) v/(4L)
Beats                     |f₁ – f₂|
Doppler                   f' = f (v ± vₒ)/(v ± vₛ)
Wave equation             y = A sin 2π(x/λ – t/T)
Transverse wave speed     v = √(T/μ)
Longitudinal (sound)      v = √(γP/ρ) = √(γRT/M)
