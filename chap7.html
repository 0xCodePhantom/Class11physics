Chapter 7 — Gravitation

One of the most beautiful and scoring chapters. Everything revolves around just two equations: Newton’s law of gravitation and gravitational potential energy. Master variation of g, orbital velocity, and escape velocity — 10–12 marks guaranteed.

Exam Hack: Memorise the “magic ratios” table (gₕ, T, escape velocity) — half the numericals are direct formula-based.

1. Newton’s Universal Law of Gravitation

F = G m₁ m₂ / r²  G = 6.67 × 10⁻¹¹ N m² kg⁻²

2. Acceleration due to Gravity (g)

At surface          g = GM/R²
At height h          gₕ = g (R/(R+h))² ≈ g (1 – 2h/R)   (h << R)
At depth d           g_d = g (1 – d/R)
At poles             gₚ > gₑ (earth is flattened)
g decreases with height, depth, and latitude (max at poles).

3. Gravitational Potential & Field

Potential V = –GM/r
Field     g⃗ = –GM/r² (radially inward)
Potential energy U = –G M m / r

4. Orbital & Escape Velocity

Orbital velocity vₒ = √(GM/R+h) ≈ √(gR) for near earth Time period T = 2π √((R+h)³)/GM) ≈ 84 min for low orbit Escape velocity vₑ = √(2GM/R) = √(2gR) ≈ 11.2 km/s from earth Energy of satellite E = –GMm/(2r) (negative → bound)
Geostationary satellite: T = 24 h, r ≈ 42,000 km, above equator

5. Kepler’s Three Laws

1. Elliptical orbit with Sun at one focus
2. Areal velocity constant (L constant)
3. T² ∝ r³  (T²/r³ = constant for all planets)

6. Must-Do Solved Numericals (Board Favourite)

Q1: Find g at height equal to radius of earth (R).

gₕ = g (R/(R+R))² = g/4 = 2.45 m/s²

Q2: Escape velocity from moon is 2.4 km/s and acceleration due to gravity on moon is g/6. Find radius of moon.

vₑ = √(2gR) ⇒ 2.4×10³ = √(2 × g/6 × R) ⇒ R = R = 1.73 × 10⁶ m

Q3: Orbital radius of geostationary satellite?

T = 24×3600 s ⇒ r³ = GMT²/(4π²) ⇒ r ≈ 42,400 km

Q4: Percentage change in g if earth stops rotating.

At equator gₑ = g – Rω² cos²λ → gₑ ≈ 0.034 m/s² decrease
% change ≈ 0.34% (negligible)

Q5: Two satellites A & B, r_A : r_B = 1:8. Ratio of their time periods?

T² ∝ r³ ⇒ T_A/T_B = (1/8)^{3/2} = 1/(8√8) = 1:22.627 or 1:2√2

7. Practice Questions

  1. Find g at a depth 1600 km (Rₑ = 6400 km).
  2. Escape velocity from earth surface?
  3. Ratio of escape velocity from surface of earth and moon if radius ratio 4:1 and density ratio 3:5.
  4. Time period of satellite at height 36000 km.

(Answers): 1) 8 m/s² 2) 11.2 km/s 3) 5:2 4) 24 h
