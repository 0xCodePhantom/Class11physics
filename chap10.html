Chapter 10 — Thermal Properties of Matter

High-weightage, super-scoring chapter (10–12 marks). Most questions are direct formula-based. Master thermal expansion coefficients, latent heat, and conduction formulas — full marks guaranteed.

Board Favourite: Linear expansion + calorimetry + rate of cooling + conduction through slab.

1. Thermal Expansion (Must Memorise)

Linear  ΔL = L₀ α ΔT  α = coefficient of linear expansion
Area   ΔA = A₀ γ ΔT  γ = 2α
Volume  ΔV = V₀ β ΔT  β = 3α 
For bimetallic strip: bends towards metal with lower α
Apparent expansion of liquid: β_apparent = β_glass + γ_liquid? No!
γ_liquid = β_apparent + β_glass

2. Specific Heat & Heat Exchange

Q = m c ΔT  c = specific heat
Q = m L  L = latent heat
Heat lost = Heat gained (no loss)

3. Conduction

Rate of heat flow  dQ/dt = KA(ΔT)/d
Thermal resistance  R = d/(KA) 
For composite slab (series):  R_total = R₁ + R₂
For parallel: 1/R_total = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂

4. Newton’s Law of Cooling

dT/dt = –k (T – T₀)  ⇒ T = T₀ + (T₁ – T₀)e⁻ᵏᵗ

5. Board-Level Solved Numericals

Q1: Steel rod length 1 m (α=12×10⁻⁶/°C) heated from 20°C to 120°C. Final length?

ΔL = LαΔT = 1 × 12×10⁻⁶ × 100 = 1.0012 m

Q2: 100 g ice at 0°C added to 200 g water at 80°C (c_water=4200 J/kgK). Final temp?

Heat released by water = 200×4.2×(80–T)
Heat to melt ice + heat water = 100×336 + 100×4.2×T
Solving → T = 53.3°C

Q3: Two slabs A (K=200 W/mK, d=2 cm) & B (K=100 W/mK, d=1 cm) in series. Rate of heat flow if ΔT=100°C, A=100 cm²?

R_total = (0.02/200) + (0.01/100) = 0.0002
Q/t = ΔT / R_total × A = 100 / 0.0002 × 0.01 = 5000 W

Q4: A body cools from 80°C to 60°C in 5 min. Room temp 30°C. Time to cool to 40°C?

Average temp diff: first 70–30=40, second 50–30=20 → rate halves → time doubles → 10 min

Q5: Clock pendulum (α=18×10⁻⁶/°C) correct at 20°C. Temp rises to 40°C. Time loss per day?

ΔT/T = ½ α Δθ → ΔT = ½ ×18×10⁻⁶ ×20 ×86400 = 15.55 s loss/day

6. Practice Questions

  1. A steel rail 10 m long at 10°C. Max gap to avoid buckling at 50°C (α_steel=12×10⁻⁶)?
  2. 50 g steam at 100°C added to 200 g ice at 0°C. Final mass of water?
  3. Find effective K of two rods of same length & area in parallel (K₁=200, K₂=400).
  4. Body cools from 70°C to 50°C in 6 min. How much more time to reach 35°C (room 25°C)?

(Answers): 1) 4.8 mm 2) 248.5 g 3) 266.67 W/mK 4) 12 min more
